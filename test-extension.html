Ancient History

In 79 AD, Mount Vesuvius erupted, destroying the cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum. This was one of the most devastating volcanic eruptions in recorded history.

Earlier, in 62 AD, a major earthquake struck Pompeii, causing significant damage to buildings and infrastructure.

The city of Pompeii was founded around 600 BC by the Oscans, an ancient Italic people.

Modern Discoveries

In 1748, systematic excavations of Pompeii began under the direction of Spanish military engineer Rocque Joaquin de Alcubierre.

In 1860, Giuseppe Fiorelli became director of excavations and introduced new archaeological methods.

In 1997, Pompeii was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Recent Research

In 2018, archaeologists discovered a new section of the city that had been buried under volcanic material.

In 2021, researchers used advanced imaging techniques to study the remains of victims preserved in the ash.
